The presence of immiscible fluids can be seen in all kinds of application, such as in the surface contact between ocean and atmosphere or at petroleum extraction industry, in which gas bubbles are formed at a high-temperature process. A numerical approach is used to discretize the fluid movement equations through the finite element method. Thereby, complex geometries can be modeled by a computational mesh of tetrahedral elements. {%- endmark %} {% mark link-%} - [read more][1] [1]: {{media_url('html/research/two-phase/text.html')}} - [publications][2] [2]: {{media_url('html/research/two-phase/publications.html')}} {%- endmark %}
